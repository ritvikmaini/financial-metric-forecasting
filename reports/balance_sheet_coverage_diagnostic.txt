Reading: AAPL FY2008/FY2015 each have 5-6 first-filing rows (10-K + early 10-Q comparatives)
  with ALL 7 BS fields populated. Later rows (years after the FY end) re-emit ONLY cash and
  total_equity as comparative facts and leave the other 5 BS fields NULL.

Coverage tool dedup: ORDER BY end_date DESC, accepted_date DESC. For a given (fy, period)
  every row shares the same end_date (it's the period-end), so end_date DESC is a no-op tie
  for these BS rows. The accepted_date DESC tiebreaker then picks the LATEST-filed row — which
  is the THINNEST comparative restatement (cash + equity only). The first-filing row with the
  full balance sheet is discarded by the dedup. This is what produces the implausibly-low
  AAPL/MSFT BS coverage in reports/raw_column_coverage.txt.
