Observation: under Apple's fiscal calendar (FY ends late September), FY2015's
Q1 ends 2014-12-27 and is filed 2015-01-28 — that is BEFORE the FY2015 10-K
accepted 2015-10-28. But the fixture stores that row under fiscal_year=2014,
not fiscal_year=2015. Meanwhile the row stored under (fiscal_year=2015, Q1,
end=2015-12-26) is actually Apple's FY2016 Q1 — filed 2016-01-27, which is
AFTER the FY2015 10-K accepted date.

Consequence for derive_q4_rows(fy=2015, accepted_cutoff=2015-10-28):
  Q1 (fy=2015 bucket): earliest accepted 2016-01-27 — UNAVAILABLE at FY filing.
  Q2 (fy=2015 bucket): earliest accepted 2015-04-28 — available.
  Q3 (fy=2015 bucket): earliest accepted 2015-07-22 — available.
derive_q4_rows requires ALL three quarters; Q1 missing -> NO Q4 emitted at the
FY filing. Q4 only emerges later, when the next year's 10-Q/10-K pushes the
(mislabeled) Q1 row into the table.

--- Part C: root-cause hypothesis ---

HYPOTHESIS (ii) — bug in normalize.py, but NOT in derive_q4_rows. The bug is
upstream in _compute_fy_end_dates / _derive_fiscal_year.

Companyfacts DOES carry discrete 90-day Q1/Q2/Q3 facts for AAPL FY2015 (12 of
them in window; fp labels Q1/Q2/Q3 from 10-Q filings AND fp=FY pieces from the
10-K's per-quarter breakout). YTD facts (~180d / ~270d) are also present
(4 total). So the YTD-only theory (i) is falsified at the source — discrete
quarter facts exist.

The actual failure: _compute_fy_end_dates iterates all facts with fp=='FY' and
form in {'10-K','10-K/A'}, stores f.end indexed by f.end.year, and takes
max() per calendar year. But AAPL's 10-K emits FIVE 90-day quarterly pieces
with fp=FY plus the 363d annual (see Part B, accn 0001193125-15-356351 filed
2015-10-28: rows with fp=FY ending 2014-09-27, 2014-12-27, 2015-03-28,
2015-06-27, 2015-09-26). For calendar year 2014, the max() across those fp=FY
ends is 2014-12-27 — but Apple's FY2014 actually ended 2014-09-27.

_derive_fiscal_year then sees the Q1 fact ending 2014-12-27 and asks: is
fy_end_dates[2014] (= 2014-12-27, the WRONG value) >= 2014-12-27? Yes — so it
labels that Q1 piece as fiscal_year=2014. Apple's real FY2015 Q1 thus lands
under fiscal_year=2014. By the same logic, FY2016 Q1 (end 2015-12-26) lands
under fiscal_year=2015.

Consequence: when derive_q4_rows runs for fy=2015 at accepted_cutoff=
2015-10-28, the only row in the (fy=2015, Q1) bucket has accepted=2016-01-27
(strictly later). Q4 derivation is skipped. Q4 only materialises at the next
year's filings.

Independent confirmation: rerunning _compute_fy_end_dates on live AAPL
companyfacts yields:
  {2006: 2006-09-30, 2007: 2007-09-29,
   2008: 2008-12-27, 2009: 2009-12-26, 2010: 2010-12-25, 2011: 2011-12-31,
   2012: 2012-12-29, 2013: 2013-12-28, 2014: 2014-12-27, 2015: 2015-12-26,
   2016: 2016-12-31, 2017: 2017-12-31, 2018: 2018-12-29, 2019: 2019-12-28,
   2020: 2020-09-26, 2021: 2021-09-25, 2022: 2022-09-24, 2023: 2023-09-30,
   2024: 2024-09-28, 2025: 2025-09-27}

FY2008-FY2019 entries are systematically wrong by ~3 months (the per-quarter
fp=FY pieces dominate the max()). FY2020+ entries are correct, which matches
Artifact 2's observed "AAPL Q4 contemporaneous from FY2021+, year-lagged
FY2009-FY2019" boundary (the FY2020 flip likely tracks a change in either
Apple's filing format or the SEC's fp tagging — pre-FY2020 10-Ks emit per-
quarter pieces with fp=FY; FY2020+ do not, or no longer in a way that beats
the 363d FY end-date in the max()).

derive_q4_rows itself is logically sound; it has nothing to work with because
upstream period-labeling sends the discrete Q1 into the wrong FY bucket.

Note also: a second symptom of the same bug is the mislabeled Q1 rows visible
in Artifact 2 (every AAPL fiscal_year's Q1 has an end_date in the NEXT
calendar year — that "Q1" is actually the next FY's Q1). The two symptoms are
the same defect viewed from different angles.

A reasonable repair direction (NOT done in this T3a probe): in
_compute_fy_end_dates, prefer the ANNUAL fp=FY fact (the 340-380d one) over
the per-quarter pieces — e.g., only collect ends from facts whose
(end - start) is in the annual window, or use min() per calendar year when
the company's fiscal year does NOT align with the calendar year (which a
secondary inference from Q3-end + ~91d would surface). This is out of scope
for T3a.
